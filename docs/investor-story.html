Slide 1 · Business Context

Insurance rating breaks down when systems cannot speak the same language.

InsuRateConnect exists because insurers still rely on fragile, point-to-point integrations between policy systems and rating engines. The result is delay, confusion, and expensive operational friction.

Without Integration Layer

An American tourist ordering food in a Chinese restaurant in Beijing.

The tourist points at pictures, guesses at the menu, and hopes the order is right. The restaurant tries to interpret what was meant. The order is wrong, everyone is frustrated, and the experience breaks down.

  • Misunderstood inputs
  • Manual guesswork
  • Wrong outcomes
  • Slow and frustrating process
With Integration Layer

The same tourist and restaurant, but now with a translator.

The translator speaks both English and Mandarin, understands American preferences and Chinese menu items, and makes sure the order is placed correctly. The customer gets the right meal. The restaurant serves with confidence.

  • Clear translation between both sides
  • Context-aware interpretation
  • Correct order, first time
  • Happy customer, happy restaurant

Slide 2 · User Flow

Users move from setup to live rating execution in one governed workflow.

The platform is designed for product, actuarial, and integration teams to configure rating logic without rebuilding integrations every time a product changes.

User Outcome

Launch and manage an insurance rating product without a new custom integration project.

The user journey starts with system setup, moves through product configuration, and ends with test execution, operational visibility, and audit-ready transaction history.

1

Register systems

Connect the source system and target rating engine that need to exchange rating data.

2

Create a product line

Define the insurance product and assign its business context, source, and destination systems.

3

Configure orchestration flow

Set the execution path for validation, mapping, rules, rating engine calls, response handling, and event publication.

4

Define mappings and rules

Translate fields between systems and apply product, state, or transaction-specific rating logic.

5

Run a test rating

Submit a sample payload and execute the configured flow through the platform.

6

Review trace and operate

Inspect step-by-step execution, troubleshoot outcomes, and monitor transaction history for audit and performance visibility.

InsuRateConnect changes the user experience from “build another custom integration” to “configure, test, and operate a rating product on a reusable platform.”
